General Principles

Throughout its long history, chess has evolved a set of fundamental principles that guide decision-making, shape middlegame strategies, and contribute to overall success on the board. Mastering these principles is essential for every chess player, regardless of skill level. This webpage is dedicated to explaining the foundational principles of chess, providing beginners with the knowledge they need to navigate the complexities of the game more effectively. By understanding and applying these basic principles, players can lay the groundwork for success and elevate their chess-playing abilities. There are many principles, but this webpage aims to address the most important ones.

King Safety

Control the Center

Coordinate The Pieces

The king is the most important piece on the board, as the game can end in checkmate. Therefore, the king must be protected at all costs in order to not lose the game.

The Center of the board is often the most critical area. Pieces and Pawns that occupy and attack the center often influence the game the most. The center allows the pieces to respond to attacks on all sides of the board, gives extra space, and allows for mobility.

No Piece can adequately attack or defend by itself. It must be supported by other pieces, by other pawns. You cannot attack when the attackers are not greater than the defenders, and the defenders cannot defend if they are weaker than the attackers. This principle encompasses other principles related to pieces.
